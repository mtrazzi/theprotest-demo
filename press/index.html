Coalition of 13 Organizations to March on OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind in Largest AI Protest in US History

San Francisco · Saturday, July 11, 2026

Stop the AI Race march, San Francisco, March 21, 2026. Photo by Rachel Shu.

San Francisco, CA — On Saturday, July 11th, protesters will march to the offices of OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind to demand a pause on the development of frontier artificial intelligence.

An unprecedented coalition of 13 organizations including Stop The AI Race, boycott groups (QuitGPT), unions (National Union of Healthcare Workers), bay area anti-surveillance groups (AI Action), and even climate groups (Food & Water Watch) will join forces in what is expected to be the largest AI protest in US history.

The Coalition

The protest is organized by Michael Trazzi, leader of Stop the AI Race, the movement he launched when he sat outside the headquarters of Google DeepMind in London, in a hunger strike calling for AI company CEOs to publicly commit to pausing frontier model development if every other company also paused.

Since the initial hunger strike, the movement has grown. On March 21st, Stop the AI Race marched on Anthropic, OpenAI, and xAI to call for a conditional pause, and was featured in the NYT, Washington Post and The Atlantic. Since then, Anthropic wrote it expects it “would slow down or temporarily pause” if other labs verifiably did too, and OpenAI wrote it expects coordination, including slowing frontier development, to become more important.

These are first steps, but “expects” isn't a commitment. Stop the AI Race demands that AI CEOs say they would pause if everyone else pauses, and lay out the concrete verification regime they'd need. So in July, they march again. AI companies claim they are building safely for the benefit of humanity. It's time they prove it.

The coalition represents a diverse range of concerns about AI development with a shared demand: pause the development of frontier AI until it can be done safely and equitably.

Speakers at the protest will include:

  • Representatives from QuitGPT and Food & Water Watch.
  • Michael Trazzi, protest organizer and documentary filmmaker.
  • Will Fithian, associate professor at UC Berkeley.
  • David Krueger, Assistant Professor in Machine Learning at Mila, University of Montreal.
  • Nate Soares, President of the Machine Intelligence Research Institute.

AI is already beginning to pose serious national security risks through its advanced cyber hacking capabilities. Last week the head of the CIA, John Ratcliffe, said that the capabilities of frontier AI were “akin to digital nuclear weapons” and that they are causing a “fundamental reshaping of the CIA's entire approach to technology.”

But many experts say that this is merely a preview of the extraordinary risks that AI could pose to society. In 2023, hundreds of public figures, AI experts and CEOs signed a public statement that “Mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war.”

The capabilities of AI are accelerating rapidly and some experts predict that we could start to lose control of these systems as early as 2027. The fundamental problem is that AI companies are developing this supremely powerful technology in a desperate and unregulated race against their competitors. Any company taking adequate care to ensure that they understand and control their AI systems would jeopardize their position in the race and risk falling behind.

AI is far too important a technology to be developed in this reckless manner. It is not like other technologies. It automates the process of thinking itself. If we are not careful, we could develop machines that outcompete us in every dimension and do not care for our safety and wellbeing. It's time to Stop the Race.

About The AI Protest

The AI Protest (theprotest.ai) brings together organizations standing against the reckless race to superintelligence. This is a nonviolent, peaceful assembly of concerned citizens, families, and researchers. Employees of AI companies are welcome to join.
