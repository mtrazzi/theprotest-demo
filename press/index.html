350 March on OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind in Largest US Anti-AI Protest

San Francisco · July 13, 2026

“The AI Protest” march, San Francisco, July 11, 2026. Photo by Rachel Bujalski.

San Francisco, July 13 — On Saturday July 11, 350 protesters marched from OpenAI to Anthropic to Google DeepMind in San Francisco, demanding AI lab CEOs commit to pausing frontier AI development if every other major lab does the same. It was roughly double of their March 21st turnout, which was previously covered in the New York Times, Washington Post, and The Atlantic.

“The AI Protest” was led by a coalition including Stop the AI Race, QuitGPT, Food & Water Watch, and the National Union of Healthcare Workers. Coalition members represent many causes: protecting the environment, advancing AI safety, preserving democracy, and curtailing corporate power.

The Coalition

“In the spring, we led 200 people to march outside all three labs,” said organizer Michael Trazzi. “Today we had about double that, and we’re going to keep coming back, bigger and bigger until they stop building AI without our consent. The most cited AI scientists say that building AI smarter than all of humanity could lead to human extinction. And it’s already hurting people in real time. So we’ve organized this protest so people can tell the AI company CEOs to commit to pausing AI development if every other major AI company, including in China, does the same.”
